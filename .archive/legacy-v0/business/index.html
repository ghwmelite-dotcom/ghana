The Ghana Advantage

Why Invest in Ghana?

Ghana offers a unique combination of stability, growth potential, and business-friendly environment.

Political Stability

Longest-standing democracy in West Africa with peaceful power transitions and a strong rule of law.

Strategic Location

Gateway to West Africa and access to ECOWAS market of 400 million consumers and AfCFTA's 1.3 billion.

Skilled Workforce

Young, educated, and English-speaking population with strong work ethic and growing talent pool.

Attractive Incentives

Tax holidays, customs exemptions, and special incentives in Free Zones and priority sectors.

Rich Natural Resources

Africa's largest gold producer, oil & gas reserves, cocoa, and abundant renewable energy potential.

Digital Infrastructure

Growing tech ecosystem, multiple submarine cables, and government digitization initiatives.

Investment Incentives

Ghana offers competitive incentives to attract and retain foreign investment.

Tax Holidays

Up to 10 years corporate tax exemption in Free Zones. Reduced rates of 1% for exports.

Customs Exemptions

Import duty exemptions on plant, machinery, and equipment for approved investments.

Profit Repatriation

100% repatriation of dividends, profits, and capital gains. No restrictions on forex transfers.

Investment Guarantees

Protection against expropriation under GIPC Act. Member of MIGA and bilateral investment treaties.

Accelerated Depreciation

Generous capital allowances and accelerated depreciation rates for industrial investments.

Location Incentives

Additional incentives for investments in regional capitals and less developed areas.

Ghana Free Zones

Free Zones offer the most competitive incentives for export-oriented businesses, with complete tax exemptions, duty-free imports, and streamlined regulatory processes.

  • 0% corporate tax for first 10 years
  • 100% exemption from import duties
  • Relief from double taxation
  • No forex controls within zones
  • Minimum investment of $200,000 (or $100,000 with Ghanaian participation)
