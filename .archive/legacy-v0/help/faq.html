🪪 Identity Documents

To apply for a Ghana Card (National ID), follow these steps:

  • Visit nia.gov.gh to pre-register online
  • Book an appointment at your nearest NIA registration center
  • Bring required documents: birth certificate, passport photo, proof of address
  • Complete biometric capture at the registration center
  • Collect your card after processing (usually 2-4 weeks)

View full Ghana Card guide →

Required documents for passport application:

  • Ghana Card (National ID)
  • Birth Certificate
  • Two recent passport photos
  • Completed application form
  • Payment of applicable fees

For renewals, you also need your old passport. Learn more →

To replace a lost Ghana Card:

  • Visit any police station to file a report and obtain an extract
  • Go to your nearest NIA district office
  • Submit the police extract and fill out a replacement form
  • Pay the replacement fee (GHS 30)
  • Your new card will be ready within 2 weeks

Passport processing times vary:

  • Regular processing: 10-15 working days
  • Express processing: 3-5 working days (additional fee)
  • Emergency: 24-48 hours (limited circumstances)

Processing times may vary during peak periods. Track your application at passport.mfa.gov.gh

💼 Business Services

To register a business in Ghana:

  • Search for and reserve your business name at RGD
  • Choose your business structure (sole proprietorship, partnership, or limited company)
  • Complete the registration form and submit required documents
  • Pay registration fees
  • Obtain your certificate of incorporation/registration

Registration can be done online at rgd.gov.gh. Full guide →

To obtain a TIN from Ghana Revenue Authority:

  • Visit gra.gov.gh and click on TIN registration
  • Fill in personal/business details
  • Submit Ghana Card number for verification
  • Your TIN will be generated instantly

TIN is required for all tax payments and business transactions. Learn more →

Ghana offers various investment incentives:

  • Tax holidays of 5-10 years in Free Zones
  • Reduced corporate tax rates in priority sectors
  • Import duty exemptions on capital equipment
  • Accelerated depreciation allowances
  • Automatic immigrant quotas for foreign investors

Visit Invest in Ghana for detailed information on incentives.

✈️ Travel & Immigration

Visa requirements depend on your nationality:

  • ECOWAS citizens: No visa required
  • African Union citizens: Visa on arrival available
  • Other nationalities: Visa required before travel

Apply online at ghana.gov.gh/visa. Full visa guide →

Steps to apply for a Ghana visa:

  • Complete the online application form
  • Upload required documents (passport, photo, itinerary)
  • Pay the visa fee online
  • Submit to nearest Ghana embassy if required
  • Receive your e-visa or collect at embassy

View complete requirements →

Vaccination requirements for travelers to Ghana:

  • Required: Yellow Fever vaccination (certificate required)
  • Recommended: Hepatitis A & B, Typhoid, Meningitis
  • Advised: Malaria prophylaxis

Consult your doctor 4-6 weeks before travel for personalized advice.

🏛️ Government Services

You can contact government ministries through:

  • Our Ministries Directory with contact details
  • The government hotline: 311
  • Official ministry websites and social media
  • Physical visits during working hours (8am-5pm)

Under the Right to Information Act:

  • Submit a written request to the relevant public institution
  • Specify the information you need clearly
  • Pay applicable processing fees if required
  • Response should be provided within 14 days
  • Appeal to the RTI Commission if denied

🏥 Health Services

To register for NHIS:

  • Visit your nearest NHIS office or accredited registration center
  • Bring your Ghana Card and passport photo
  • Complete the registration form
  • Pay the premium (free for children under 18, pregnant women, elderly 70+, SSNIT contributors)
  • Receive your NHIS card

Renew annually to maintain coverage.

Birth Registration:

  • Must be done within 1 year of birth (free)
  • Visit Births & Deaths Registry with hospital notification form
  • Parents' Ghana Cards required

Death Registration:

  • Must be done within 3 months
  • Bring medical certificate of cause of death
  • Deceased's Ghana Card and informant's ID

🎓 Education

To enroll in public school:

  • Visit the school during enrollment period (usually January-March)
  • Bring child's birth certificate and immunization records
  • Parent/guardian Ghana Card
  • Proof of residence
  • Previous school records (for transfers)

Free Compulsory Basic Education covers Kindergarten to JHS.

Certificate verification services:

  • WASSCE/BECE: Contact WAEC Ghana
  • University degrees: Contact the issuing university
  • Professional certificates: Contact relevant professional body

Fees apply for verification services. Process usually takes 5-10 working days.

🤝 Social Services

Social Security registration:

  • Employers must register employees within 30 days of employment
  • Visit any SSNIT office with Ghana Card
  • Self-employed persons can register voluntarily
  • Contribution: 13.5% of salary (employer 13%, employee 5.5%)

Check your contributions at ssnit.org.gh

Livelihood Empowerment Against Poverty (LEAP) eligibility:

  • Extremely poor households
  • Must have orphans, elderly (65+), or persons with disabilities
  • Contact your District Social Welfare Office
  • Household assessment will be conducted
  • If eligible, receive bi-monthly cash transfers
