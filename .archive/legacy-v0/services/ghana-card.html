Benefits

Why You Need a Ghana Card

The Ghana Card is essential for accessing government services and more

National Identification

Your official identity document recognized across all government agencies and institutions.

Banking & Finance

Required for opening bank accounts, mobile money registration, and financial services.

SIM Registration

Mandatory for registering and re-registering mobile phone SIM cards.

Healthcare Access

Links to your NHIS membership for seamless healthcare services.

Passport Application

Required document for applying or renewing your Ghanaian passport.

E-Government Services

Digital authentication for online government services and e-signatures.

How It Works

Registration Process

Simple steps to get your Ghana Card

1

Pre-Register Online (Optional)

Visit the NIA website or use the GhanaCard App to pre-register and fill in your details. This speeds up the process at the registration center.

2

Visit Registration Center

Go to your nearest NIA registration center with your supporting documents. Centers are available in all 261 districts across Ghana.

3

Biometric Capture

Your fingerprints, photograph, and signature will be captured digitally. This information is stored securely on your Ghana Card chip.

4

Receive Application Slip

You'll receive a slip with your Personal Identification Number (PIN) and collection information. Keep this safe!

5

Collect Your Card

Return to the registration center after 2-4 weeks with your slip to collect your Ghana Card. You may also opt for home delivery.

What You Need

Registration Requirements

Documents and information needed for registration

Birth Certificate

Original birth certificate from Births & Deaths Registry

Parent/Guardian Details

Ghana Card numbers of both parents (for new registrations)

Proof of Address

Utility bill, bank statement, or Ghana Post GPS address

Old ID (If Available)

Voter's ID, Passport, or Driver's License for verification
