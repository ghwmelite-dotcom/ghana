What Awaits You

Immerse yourself in authentic Ghanaian experiences that will stay with you forever.

Festivals & Culture

Experience vibrant festivals like Homowo, Aboakyir, and the famous Chale Wote Street Art Festival.

Wildlife & Safari

Spot elephants at Mole National Park, Ghana's largest wildlife refuge with diverse fauna.

Cuisine & Markets

Taste jollof rice, kelewele, and waakye while exploring bustling markets like Makola.

Walk through history at slave castles, Ashanti palaces, and the W.E.B. Du Bois Centre.

Traveler's Guide

Practical Information

Everything you need to know for a smooth and enjoyable visit.

Currency

Ghana Cedi (GHS). ATMs widely available. Major credit cards accepted in cities.

Language

English (official). Twi, Ga, Ewe, Hausa also widely spoken across regions.

Climate

Tropical. Dry season Nov-Mar (best for travel). Rainy season Apr-Oct.

Time Zone

GMT (UTC+0). No daylight saving time observed.

Electricity

230V, 50Hz. UK-style plugs (Type G). Bring adapter if needed.

Emergency

  • Police: 191
  • Fire: 192
  • Ambulance: 193
  • General: 112
