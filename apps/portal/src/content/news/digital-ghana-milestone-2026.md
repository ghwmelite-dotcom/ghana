The Ghana.GOV platform crossed 2 million registered citizen accounts on 1 April
2026, marking a significant milestone in the government's digital transformation
agenda. The platform, powered by GhanaConnect — Ghana's national digital
identity and authentication service — allows citizens to access government
services without visiting a physical office.

In the first quarter of 2026 alone, citizens completed 1.3 million service
transactions on the platform. The most-used services were passport renewal
pre-qualification, Ghana Revenue Authority (GRA) tax clearance certificate
requests, and National Health Insurance Authority (NHIA) card renewal. Mobile
access accounts for 87 percent of all sessions, consistent with Ghana's
predominantly mobile internet usage.

The Ministry of Communications and Digitalisation has set a target of 5 million
registered accounts by December 2026. To support this, the GhanaConnect
enrolment process will be available offline at all 292 National Identification
Authority (NIA) registration centres and at all Ghana Post offices nationwide.

Citizens who have not yet registered can visit any Ghana Post branch or NIA
centre with their Ghana Card to create an account. Assistance is available in
English, Twi, Ewe, Ga, and Dagbani.
